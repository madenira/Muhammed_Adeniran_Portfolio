Undergraduate Research Case Study

Security Framework for Self-Driving Networks

A research assistant project focused on intrusion detection, secure communication protocols, and real-time threat mitigation strategies for self-driving networks.

Project Overview

As an Undergraduate Research Assistant at the University of North Carolina at Pembroke, I worked on a security framework for self-driving networks. The work focused on improving security, communication, and real-time response in networked environments.

What I Worked On

  • Developed and implemented a security framework for self-driving networks.
  • Focused on intrusion detection and real-time threat mitigation strategies.
  • Studied secure communication protocols for networked systems.
  • Supported research goals through technical investigation and documentation.
  • Connected cybersecurity concepts with modern network automation and intelligent infrastructure.

Project Outcomes

  • Gained undergraduate research experience in cybersecurity and network security.
  • Built stronger interest in secure systems, information systems, and graduate-level research.
  • Strengthened technical communication and research preparation.
